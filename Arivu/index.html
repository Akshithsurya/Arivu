The Core Problem

Two paths.
Most others never mentioned.

Most +2 school students in Palakkad hear about two futures when pursuing STEM: IIT-JEE or NEET. Not because those are the only options but because the information gap is real and wide.

"You can't aim for something you've never heard of. The problem isn't IIT or NEET. It's that most students here never get to see the full map."

Arivu is a bilingual science career literacy project. A website, a WhatsApp newsletter, and will be a direct school outreach. Built by two students from Palakkad, for students from Palakkad.

Kerala Heritage

The first calculus
was written here

Long before Leibniz or Newton, mathematicians in Kerala were working with infinite series. This heritage isn't decorative. It's evidence.

Mādhava's series for π, c. 1400 CE
c. 1340 – 1425 CE
Mādhava of Saṅgamagrāma
Irinjalakuda, Kerala
Derived infinite series for sine, cosine, and arctangent, foundations for what became calculus. His series for pi predates Leibniz by nearly 300 years. Considered the originator of the Kerala School of Mathematics.
Founder of Kerala calculus tradition
1444 – 1544 CE

Exam Calendar · 2025–26

When the doors
actually open.

A complete timeline of every major STEM entrance exam in India — JEST, GATE, JAM, IIT-JEE, NEET, IISER IAT, NEST, ISI, TIFR GS, CSIR NET, CUET and ICAR. With registration windows, eligibility, and direct links to official syllabi. One PDF. Print it, pin it, plan around it.

Most students in Palakkad first hear about an exam after its registration has already closed. That's the gap this calendar exists to close. Every major STEM entrance route in India — undergraduate, postgraduate, and research-level — is listed with its registration window, exam date, eligibility, and a direct link to the official syllabus.

The PDF is built to be printed. Paper, not phone. A wall planner that lives next to your study desk, with all 13 exams and their typical months on one sheet. The downloadable file is updated each year as NTA / IIT / IISER notifications come out.

Why This Project Is Grounded
Named community: to school students in Palakkad so it's specific, reachable and real.
Documented gap: The information asymmetry in STEM career exposure here is real and defensible.
Actual credentials: NASA Space Apps nomination, quantum physics mentorship, these are verified.
Honest scope: Static site + planned school visits + monthly newsletter. Two students. Zero budget.
Rooted locally: Malayalam content, Kerala School of Mathematics. Not imported from elsewhere.
From here: We went to school in this district. That's the point.
Why we started this.
"We both had things that most students here don't: access to information, mentorship and wide resources in English. Arivu is our attempt to make some of that available in Malayalam, for students in the same +2 schools we came through. Not a polished mission statement. Just what's true."
Akshith & Aadi, co-founders
