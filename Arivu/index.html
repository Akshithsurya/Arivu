The Core Problem

Two paths.
Most others never mentioned.

Most +2 school students in Palakkad hear about two futures when pursuing STEM: IIT-JEE or NEET. Not because those are the only options but because the information gap is real and wide.

"You can't aim for something you've never heard of. The problem isn't IIT or NEET.. it's that most students here never get to see the full map."

Arivu is a bilingual science career literacy project. A website, a WhatsApp newsletter, and will be a direct school outreach. Built by two students from Palakkad, for students from Palakkad.

Scientific Heritage

Before Newton.
Before Leibniz.
Kerala had calculus.

Mathematicians from Kerala were solving problems that would not be formally solved in Europe for another two centuries. This isn't trivia, it's context for who you are.

Mādhava-Leibniz Series · Kerala School of Mathematics · c. 1380 CE · ~300 years before Leibniz
c. 1340 – 1425 CE
Mādhava of Saṅgamagrāma
Irinjalakuda, Kerala
Derived infinite series for sine, cosine, and arctangent, foundations for what became calculus. His series for pi predates Leibniz by nearly 300 years. Considered the originator of the Kerala School of Mathematics.
Founder of Kerala calculus tradition
1444 – 1544 CE
Nīlakaṇṭha Somayājī
Trikkantiyur, Kerala
Proposed a partial heliocentric planetary model decades before Copernicus published his own. Also derived an improved series expansion for pi. A mathematician, astronomer and linguist working from Kerala.
Pre-Copernican heliocentrism
c. 1500 – 1610 CE
Jyeṣhadeva
Kerala
Author of Yuktibhāṣā, widely regarded as the world's first calculus textbook, written in Malayalam prose. Rigorous proofs in vernacular language. Serious mathematics done in the people's own tongue.
World's first calculus text in Malayalam
The first calculus textbook was written in Malayalam. Arivu carries that forward by making science accessible in the vernacular, for students from a place with one of the great mathematical traditions in history. Akshith and Aadi, we both grew up here. The language is not a barrier to this work. It is the point of it.

"The first calculus textbook was written in Malayalam. The one we grew up speaking."
